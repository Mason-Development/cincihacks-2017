About CincyHacks

CincyHacks is Cincinnati's first hackathon dedicated to high school students.


When & where is CincyHacks?

CincyHacks is being hosted at the University of Cincinnati's College of Engineering and Applied Science Library from Saturday, November 4th to Sunday, November 5th!

What should I bring?

At the minimum, you'll need a student ID, laptop, charger. We also recommend bringing a change of clothes, toiletries, and a sleeping bag or pillow. You won't be able to leave CincyHacks after the event starts, so make sure you have any medications you'll need.

I've never coded!

Great! No prior knowledge is required, you're more than welcome to learn as you go!

What's a hackathon?

A hackathon is a type of coding marathon. Anyone with an interest in computer programming is able to come and work on projects for 24 hours straight, forming teams and sharing knowledge with each other. You donâ€™t have to be a programmer, or even know how to code!

Who can come?

Any current high school is welcome to come to CincyHacks--all we ask is that you bring a school issued ID with you.

Is CincyHacks free?

Yes, CincyHacks is completely free! We will be providing food, swag, snacks, and drinks at no cost.

Is there a code of conduct?

CincyHacks follows the MLH Code of Conduct.

What else is at CincyHacks?

CincyHacks will have several booths where you can meet representatives from companies interested in helping students pursue technology. We will also have lightning talks about technology!

How long is CincyHacks?

You will have 24 hours to work on your projects at CincyHacks. However, we will have activities before and after the block of coding, so expect to be there from Saturday afternoon to Sunday evening.
